About HDI
Methodology, formulas & classifications

What is the Human Development Index (HDI)?

The Human Development Index (HDI) is a summary measure of average achievement in key dimensions of human development: a long and healthy life, being knowledgeable, and having a decent standard of living. It was created by Mahbub ul Haq and Amartya Sen, and launched in 1990 by the United Nations Development Programme (UNDP).

The HDI emphasizes that people and their capabilities should be the ultimate criteria for assessing the development of a country, not economic growth alone.

The Calculation Methodology

Four metrics are transformed into three normalized dimension indices with values between 0 and 1:

Health Dimension Index

Measured by life expectancy at birth, relative to minimum (20 years) and maximum (85 years) thresholds.

Health Index = (Life Expectancy - 20) / (85 - 20)

Education Dimension Index

Measured by Expected Years of Schooling and Mean Years of Schooling. The arithmetic mean yields the dimension index.

Education Index = [ (Mean Schooling / 15) + (Expected Schooling / 18) ] / 2

Income Dimension Index

Measured by GNI per capita (PPP in USD) on a logarithmic scale reflecting diminishing marginal utility.

Income Index = [ ln(GNI per capita) - ln(100) ] / [ ln(75,000) - ln(100) ]

Combined Geometric HDI Score

The final HDI is the geometric mean of the three dimension indices.

HDI = ³√( Health Index × Education Index × Income Index )

HDI Classifications

UNDP divides countries into four tiers based on geometric scores:

Very High Development

Score ≥ 0.800

High-tech industries, advanced medical structures, extensive college attendance.

High Development

Score: 0.700 – 0.799

Solid infrastructure, rising middle class, comprehensive secondary schooling.

Medium Development

Score: 0.550 – 0.699

Developing industrial capabilities, expanding school coverage.

Low Development

Score < 0.550

Infrastructure gaps, high poverty, low literacy, constrained healthcare.
